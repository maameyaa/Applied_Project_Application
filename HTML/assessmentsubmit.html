Before Teaching
0.1. The teacher has the English lesson plan developed and ready to use in the classroom.
0.2. The classroom has English teaching aids: Big books, conventional posters, etc.
0.3. The classroom has pupil seating arrangements organized in rows and pairs

1. During Teaching: Revision
1.1. The teacher begins instruction with review that includes student interaction
1.2. The teacher introduces the lesson and tells the students what they learn
1.3. Total time for revision:

2. During Teaching: Phonemic Awareness (PA)
2.1. The teacher provides examples in English language WITHOUT print letters or words.
2.2. The teacher uses rhymes, songs, or poetry to teach the sounds and patterns of word.
2.3. Total time for PA:

3. During Teaching: Vocabulary
3.1. The teacher provides examples in English language WITHOUT print letters or words.
3.2. The teacher used conventional posters to practice new vocabulary words
3.3. Total time for Vocabulary:

4. During Teaching: Comprehension and Communication
4.1. The teacher reads a story to pupils and asks questions.
4.2. The teacher does NOT ask students to read or repeat in chorus any part of the text.
4.3. The teacher calls on students to retell parts of the story.
4.4. The teacher calls on students to use English words in a sentence of their own.
4.3. Total time for comprehension:

5. During Teaching: Generl Observations
5.1. The teacher reads a story to pupils and asks questions
5.2. The teacher does NOT ask students to read or repeat in chorus any part of the text
5.3. The teacher calls on student to retell parts of the story
5.4. The teacher calls on student to use the new English words in a sentence of their own

6. After Teaching: Reflection Notes
6.1 The teacher refers to the lesson plan during teaching
6.2 The teacher uses Akuapem Twi to facilitate explanations for the English activities
